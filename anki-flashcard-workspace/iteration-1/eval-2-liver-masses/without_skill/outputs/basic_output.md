# Liver Masses: Hemangioma vs Metastasis - Basic Flashcards

| Front | Back | Number |
|---|---|---|
| What is the most common benign liver tumor? | Cavernous hemangioma. | 1 |
| What T2-weighted MRI signal is characteristic of cavernous hemangioma? | "Light bulb" bright signal due to slow-flowing blood. | 2 |
| What enhancement pattern is typical of hemangioma on contrast-enhanced CT/MRI? | Peripheral nodular discontinuous enhancement with progressive centripetal fill-in on delayed phases. | 3 |
| Do hemangiomas demonstrate restricted diffusion on DWI? | No. | 4 |
| What enhancement pattern do hypervascular metastases (e.g., RCC, neuroendocrine, melanoma) show? | Early arterial phase enhancement with washout on portal venous and delayed phases. | 5 |
| Do metastases demonstrate restricted diffusion on DWI? | Yes. | 6 |
| What T2-weighted MRI finding is characteristic of colorectal liver metastases? | Target appearance (central T2 hyperintensity with peripheral rim). | 7 |
| In what background liver condition does hepatocellular carcinoma typically occur? | Cirrhotic livers. | 8 |
| What enhancement pattern is seen in hepatocellular carcinoma? | Arterial phase hyperenhancement with portal venous or delayed phase washout. | 9 |
| What capsular finding often accompanies hepatocellular carcinoma? | Pseudocapsule. | 10 |
