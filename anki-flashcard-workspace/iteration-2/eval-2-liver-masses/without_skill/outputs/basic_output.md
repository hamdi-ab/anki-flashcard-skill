# Basic Flashcards: Liver Masses — Hemangioma vs Metastasis

## Basic Cards

| Front | Back | Number |
|---|---|---|
| What is the most common benign liver tumor? | Cavernous hemangioma. | 1 |
| What T2-weighted MRI finding is characteristic of cavernous hemangioma? | "Light bulb" bright signal due to slow-flowing blood. | 2 |
| Describe the enhancement pattern of hemangioma on contrast CT/MRI. | Peripheral nodular discontinuous enhancement with progressive centripetal fill-in. | 3 |
| Do hemangiomas demonstrate restricted diffusion on DWI? | No. | 4 |
| What DWI finding differentiates metastases from hemangiomas? | Metastases demonstrate restricted diffusion. | 5 |
| What T2-weighted appearance is characteristic of colorectal liver metastases? | Target appearance (central T2 hyperintensity with peripheral rim). | 6 |
| What is the enhancement pattern of hypervascular metastases? | Early arterial phase enhancement with washout on portal venous and delayed phases. | 7 |
| In what clinical context does hepatocellular carcinoma typically occur? | Cirrhotic livers. | 8 |
| What enhancement pattern is characteristic of HCC? | Arterial phase hyperenhancement with portal venous or delayed phase washout. | 9 |
