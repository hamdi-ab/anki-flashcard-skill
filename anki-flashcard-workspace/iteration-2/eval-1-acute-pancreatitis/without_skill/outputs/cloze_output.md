# Acute Pancreatitis: Cloze Flashcards

| Statements | Number |
|---|---|
| {{c1::Contrast-enhanced CT}} is the imaging modality of choice for evaluating acute pancreatitis. | 1 |
| Pancreatic {{c1::necrosis}} is identified by lack of contrast enhancement after bolus contrast administration. | 2 |
| The {{c1::CT Severity Index (CTSI)}} combines the Balthazar grade with the extent of pancreatic necrosis. | 3 |
| Acute peripancreatic fluid collections (APFCs) are {{c1::non-encapsulated}} aggregations of fluid in the pancreatic bed. | 4 |
| APFCs occur within the first {{c1::4 weeks}} of onset. | 5 |
| After 4 weeks, APFCs may organize into {{c1::walled-off necrosis (WON)}} or pseudocysts. | 6 |
| Pseudocysts have a {{c1::well-defined capsule}}. | 7 |
| Gas within a necrotic collection suggests {{c1::infected necrosis}}, which requires intervention. | 8 |
| Pseudoaneurysm formation most commonly involves the {{c1::splenic artery}}. | 9 |
| Venous thrombosis in acute pancreatitis most commonly involves the {{c1::splenic vein}}. | 10 |
