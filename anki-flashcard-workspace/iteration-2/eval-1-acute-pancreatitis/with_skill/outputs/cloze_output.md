| Contrast-enhanced {{c1::CT}} is the imaging modality of choice for evaluating acute pancreatitis. | 1 |
| Necrosis in pancreatitis is identified by {{c1::lack of contrast enhancement}} after bolus contrast administration. | 2 |
| The severity of pancreatitis is graded using the {{c1::CT Severity Index (CTSI)}}, which combines the Balthazar grade with the extent of pancreatic necrosis. | 3 |
| Acute peripancreatic fluid collections (APFCs) occur within the first {{c1::4 weeks}} of onset and are non-encapsulated aggregations of fluid. | 4 |
| After 4 weeks, fluid collections may organize into {{c1::walled-off necrosis (WON)}} or pseudocysts, which have a well-defined capsule. | 5 |
| Gas within a necrotic collection suggests {{c1::infected necrosis}}, which requires intervention. | 6 |
| Pseudoaneurysm formation in pancreatitis most commonly involves the {{c1::splenic artery}}. | 7 |
| Venous thrombosis in pancreatitis most commonly involves the {{c1::splenic vein}}. | 8 |
