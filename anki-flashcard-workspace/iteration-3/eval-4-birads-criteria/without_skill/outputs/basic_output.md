## BI-RADS Assessment Categories — Basic Cards

| Front | Back | Number |
|---|---|---|
| What is BI-RADS Category 0? | Incomplete — Need additional imaging evaluation before final assessment. | 0 |
| What is BI-RADS Category 1? | Negative — No findings. Routine screening continues at standard interval. | 1 |
| What is BI-RADS Category 2? | Benign — Definitely benign finding. Routine screening. | 2 |
| What risk percentage defines BI-RADS Category 3? | Less than 2% risk of malignancy. | 3 |
| What management is recommended for BI-RADS Category 3? | Short-interval follow-up (typically 6 months); downgrade to Category 2 if stable over 2-3 years. | 3 |
| What is BI-RADS Category 4? | Suspicious — Biopsy should be considered. Subcategorized into 4A, 4B, 4C. | 4 |
| What risk defines BI-RADS 4A? | Low suspicion, 2-10% likelihood of malignancy. | 4A |
| What risk defines BI-RADS 4B? | Intermediate suspicion, 10-50% likelihood of malignancy. | 4B |
| What risk defines BI-RADS 4C? | Moderate concern, 50-95% likelihood of malignancy. | 4C |
| What is BI-RADS Category 5? | Highly Suggestive of Malignancy — at least 95% chance. Biopsy is mandatory. | 5 |
| What is BI-RADS Category 6? | Known Biopsy-Proven Malignancy — exams after biopsy confirmation, before definitive treatment. | 6 |
