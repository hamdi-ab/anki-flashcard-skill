# BI-RADS Assessment Categories — Basic (Front/Back)

## Flashcards

| Front | Back | Number |
| --- | --- | --- |
| What additional evaluation does BI-RADS Category 0 (Incomplete) require? | Additional imaging such as spot compression, magnification views, or ultrasound before a final assessment. | 1 |
| What management does BI-RADS Category 1 (Negative) require? | Routine screening at the standard interval; no additional workup. | 2 |
| What BI-RADS category describes definitely benign findings such as involuting calcifications and fat-containing masses? | Category 2 (Benign). | 3 |
| What risk of malignancy does a BI-RADS Category 3 (Probably Benign) finding carry? | Less than 2%. | 4 |
| What follow-up is required for a BI-RADS Category 3 (Probably Benign) finding? | Short-interval follow-up at 6 months; downgrade to Category 2 if stable over 2–3 years. | 5 |
| What are the three subcategories of BI-RADS Category 4 (Suspicious) and their malignancy risk ranges? | 4A (2–10%), 4B (10–50%), and 4C (50–95%). | 6 |
| What likelihood of malignancy does BI-RADS Category 5 (Highly Suggestive of Malignancy) indicate? | At least 95%; biopsy is mandatory. | 7 |
| When is BI-RADS Category 6 (Known Biopsy-Proven Malignancy) used? | For exams performed after biopsy confirmation but before definitive surgical treatment. | 8 |
