# BI-RADS Assessment Categories — Cloze Deletions

## Statements

| Statements | Number |
| --- | --- |
| BI-RADS {{c1::Category 0 (Incomplete)}} is assigned when an abnormality requires additional imaging such as spot compression or ultrasound before a final assessment. | 1 |
| BI-RADS {{c1::Category 1 (Negative)}} describes symmetric breasts with no masses, architectural distortion, or suspicious calcifications. | 2 |
| BI-RADS {{c1::Category 2 (Benign)}} includes findings such as involuting calcifications and fat-containing masses like oil cysts or lipomas. | 3 |
| A {{c1::Category 3 (Probably Benign)}} finding carries less than {{c2::2%}} risk of malignancy. | 4 |
| Category 3 (Probably Benign) requires short-interval follow-up at {{c1::6 months}} and is downgraded to Category 2 if {{c2::stable over 2–3 years}}. | 5 |
| BI-RADS {{c1::Category 4 (Suspicious)}} is subdivided into 4A, 4B, and 4C by likelihood of malignancy. | 6 |
| Category 4A has {{c1::2–10%}}, 4B has {{c1::10–50%}}, and 4C has {{c1::50–95%}} likelihood of malignancy. | 7 |
| BI-RADS {{c1::Category 5 (Highly Suggestive of Malignancy)}} carries at least {{c2::95%}} chance of malignancy with mandatory biopsy. | 8 |
| {{c1::BI-RADS Category 6 (Known Biopsy-Proven Malignancy)}} is used for exams performed after biopsy confirmation but before definitive surgical treatment. | 9 |
