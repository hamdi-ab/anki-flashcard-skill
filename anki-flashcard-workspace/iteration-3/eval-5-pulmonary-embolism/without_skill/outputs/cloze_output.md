# Cloze Deletions — Pulmonary Embolism Imaging

| Statements | Number |
|---|---|
| {{c1::CT pulmonary angiography (CTPA)}} is the first-line imaging modality for suspected acute pulmonary embolism. | 1 |
| A technically adequate CTPA requires opacification of the pulmonary trunk to at least {{c1::250 Hounsfield units}}. | 2 |
| Thrombus appears as a {{c1::filling defect}} within the opacified pulmonary artery lumen. | 3 |
| Acute emboli typically lodge at {{c1::arterial bifurcation points}}. | 4 |
| Acute emboli form {{c1::acute}} angles with the vessel wall. | 5 |
| A "saddle" embolus is lodged at the {{c1::main pulmonary artery bifurcation}}. | 6 |
| Right ventricular enlargement on CT is defined by an RV:LV ratio greater than {{c1::1.0}} on reconstructed four-chamber views. | 7 |
| Right heart strain on CT causes bowing of the interventricular septum toward the {{c1::left ventricle}}. | 8 |
| Reflux of contrast into the IVC and hepatic veins is a sign of {{c1::right heart strain}}. | 9 |
| Main pulmonary artery dilation greater than {{c1::29 mm}} indicates right heart strain. | 10 |
| {{c1::Central}} emboli (main, lobar) carry higher mortality than peripheral emboli. | 11 |
| Chronic thromboembolic disease shows {{c1::webs, bands, and intimal irregularity}} within the pulmonary arteries. | 12 |
| Chronic PE often demonstrates {{c1::mosaic attenuation}} on CT chest. | 13 |
| Chronic emboli form {{c1::obtuse}} angles with the vessel wall. | 14 |
| Chronic emboli may show {{c1::calcification}}. | 15 |
| Chronic thromboembolic pulmonary hypertension may benefit from {{c1::pulmonary thromboendarterectomy (PEA)}}. | 16 |
| Distinguishing chronic from acute PE pattern is critical for {{c1::surgical planning}}. | 17 |
