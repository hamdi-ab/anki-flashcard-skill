# Basic Flashcards — Renal Mass Characterization

| Front | Back | Number |
|---|---|---|
| What CT attenuation does AML show pre-contrast? | Negative HU (macroscopic fat). | 1 |
| What CT attenuation does RCC show pre-contrast? | 20-70 HU (soft tissue). | 2 |
| What CT attenuation does a Simple Cyst show pre-contrast? | 0-20 HU (water density). | 3 |
| What CT attenuation does Oncocytoma show pre-contrast? | 30-50 HU. | 4 |
| What is the enhancement pattern of AML on CT? | Minimal (vascular smooth muscle). | 5 |
| What is the enhancement pattern of RCC on CT? | Heterogeneous, avid. | 6 |
| What is the enhancement pattern of Simple Cyst on CT? | None. | 7 |
| What is the enhancement pattern of Oncocytoma on CT? | Homogeneous, avid with central scar. | 8 |
| What is the MRI T1 signal of AML? | High (fat). | 9 |
| What is the MRI T1 signal of RCC? | Isointense to hypointense. | 10 |
| What is the MRI T1 signal of Simple Cyst? | Homogeneous low signal. | 11 |
| What is the MRI T1 signal of Oncocytoma? | Isointense. | 12 |
| What is the MRI T2 signal of AML? | High (fat) with chemical shift. | 13 |
| What is the MRI T2 signal of RCC? | Heterogeneous high. | 14 |
| What is the MRI T2 signal of Simple Cyst? | Homogeneous very high (CSF-like). | 15 |
| What is the MRI T2 signal of Oncocytoma? | Isointense to slightly high. | 16 |
| What is the key differentiating feature of AML? | Macroscopic fat on CT or chemical shift MRI. | 17 |
| What are key differentiating features of RCC? | Pseudocapsule, tumor thrombus, metastases. | 18 |
| What is the key differentiating feature of Simple Cyst? | Thin imperceptible wall, no septa. | 19 |
| What is the key differentiating feature of Oncocytoma? | Central stellate scar on delayed imaging. | 20 |
| What is the hemorrhage risk of AML? | High (>4 cm; Wunderlich syndrome). | 21 |
| What is the hemorrhage risk of RCC? | Moderate. | 22 |
| What is the hemorrhage risk of Simple Cyst? | None. | 23 |
| What is the hemorrhage risk of Oncocytoma? | Low. | 24 |
| What is the growth pattern of AML? | Slow, indolent. | 25 |
| What is the growth pattern of RCC? | Variable (can be aggressive). | 26 |
| What is the growth pattern of Simple Cyst? | None. | 27 |
| What is the growth pattern of Oncocytoma? | Benign, may grow slowly. | 28 |
